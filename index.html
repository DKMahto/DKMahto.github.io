About Me

I am a research scholar at the Malviya National Institute of Technology (MNIT) Jaipur, India. Before joining MNIT, I pursued M. Tech. degree in Power System Engineering at the Birla Institute of Technology Mesra, Ranchi in 2017-19. I defended my Master thesis titled “Online identification of coherent generators in multi machine power systems”. In 2019, I joined the DAV Institute of Engineering & Technology as Assistant Professor. In my PhD studies, I am exploring data driven modelling applications applied to optimal power flow in active distribution network. Motivated by outstanding success of deep learning approaches for creating a more decentralized and intelligent energy paradigm while integrating high intelligence in supervisory and operational decision making. I am working in research group of Dr. Akhilesh Mathur & Prof. Rajesh Kumar in Robotics and machine analytics (Raman Lab).

Area Of Research

Data-driven Modelling

Data-driven models finds the relationships between the system state variables without explicit knowledge of the physical behavior of the system.

Deep Learning

Deep learning is a part of broader family of machine learning methods based on artificial neural networks with representation learning.

Optimal Power Flow

OPF problem seeks to control the generation/consumption of generators/loads to optimize certain objectives like Operation & Control Planning problems.
